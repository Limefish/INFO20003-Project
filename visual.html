Visual Analysis

Some brief representations and analysis of trends for comic book character demographics

Dude, The Gender Gap is Real

Males make up more than 70% of existing comic book characters.

Nowhere close to gender parity.

Good News Everyone: The Gap's Decreasing

Bad News: It's Plateauing Too Early

At this rate, a ratio that is close to being 1:1 would not be achievable in any time soon

Good girls not gone bad

The comic book industry tends to idealise female characters

50.8% of female characters played the good role versus 33.9% for males. 53.4% of males played the bad role versus 32.1%.

Gay is Good. Literally

Speaking of alignment, gay characters are more than twice as likely to have a good alignment compared to straight characters.

Bisexuals, on the other hand, tend to be neutral.

Is there a stereotype that flexbility in sexual preferences implies a flexibility in moral alignment?.

When they say sexual minority, they mean it

Although representation of homosexuality is increasing slowly over the years, they still only make up 0.5% of the total amount of characters.

The incidence of bisexuality on the other hand, remains relatively the same.

Suggests that efforts have been focused on representing just homosexuality, and not other sexual minorities.

Good Characters tend to have Public Identities

Good Characters would normally like their fans to remember their good deeds by revealing their identities.

Bivariate analysis to determine if the proportion of public identities varies with the proportion of good characters.

There is a weak positive correlation between the number of characters with public identities and the number of good characters.
